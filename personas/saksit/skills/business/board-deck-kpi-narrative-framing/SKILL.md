# Board & KPI Deck Narrative Framing

## Trigger
- Building a quarterly or annual board presentation
- Running an executive QBR or leadership review deck
- Reviewing a deck and it feels "report-y" instead of decision-ready
- Converting raw metrics into a narrative a board can act on

---

## Step-by-Step Workflow

### 1. Anchor on the decision before opening the template
Write down the single most important decision the board needs to make (or the three things they need to say "yes" to). Every slide must serve this decision.

### 2. Work backward from the deadline
- **T-7 days**: Data final, draft deck locked
- **T-10 days**: All metric owners confirm numbers and commentary
- **T-14 days**: Outlined and assigned

If the deck isn’t locked until the night before, it’s a process failure, not a slide failure.

### 3. Standard slide order (8–10 slides)
1. **Executive Summary** — the "movie trailer"
2. **Key Metrics Dashboard / Heat Map** — North-Star KPI strip (ARR, NRR/GRR, burn, runway, CAC, pipeline)
3. **Financial Update** — P&L, cash, balance-sheet highlights, variance bridge
4. **Revenue & Pipeline** — bookings, conversion, coverage, segment health
5. **Product / Unit Economics** — roadmap, adoption, CM1/CM2/CM3 if relevant
6. **GTM / Growth** — channel mix, CAC, efficiency scores, headcount plan
7. **People & Org** — key hires, structure, culture / engagement score
8. **Risks & Issues** — top 3–5 risks with owner and mitigation
9. **Asks & Next Steps** — explicit decisions, budget requests, policy votes
10. **Appendix** — backup tables. Move non-essential detail here.

### 4. Pyramid Principle — answer first, then prove it
Structure every slide with:
- **Action title** (the insight)
- **Key takeaway** (one sentence)
- **Support** (1–3 charts or bullets)

Never put a bare topic title like "Q3 Pipeline" on a slide. Titles must be claims: "Q3 pipeline dropped 18% due to late-cycle delays in Enterprise."

### 5. The 3-3-3 Executive Summary (or North-Star + Asks)
Spend disproportionate time on the first slide. Use:

**Option A — 3-3-3 Framework**
- **Three things that went well** (headline + metric)
- **Three things at risk** (variance explanation, not excuse)
- **Three things we need from the board** (explicit asks, decisions, or approvals)

**Option B — North-Star + Drivers**
- Single headline number (e.g., "ARR $14.2M, NRR 128%, 8 months runway")
- Top 3 drivers behind the number
- Top 3 asks

### 6. Metrics dashboard rules
- Strip: **Health → Trend → Segment**
- Show Current, Plan, Prior, and YoY on one axis
- RAG status (Green/Amber/Red) only if criteria are pre-defined
- If a metric is off plan, the accompanying text must explain *why* before the board asks.

### 7. Variance bridge, not variance excuse
For every material miss:
1. **Plan / Actual / Variance**
2. **Bridge**: what caused the miss (volume, price, timing, one-time)
3. **Correction**: what we’re doing about it
4. **Updated forecast**

Formula: `Variance % = (Actual - Plan) / Plan`

### 8. Risks: quantity and quality
- Max 5 risks on the main deck (rest in appendix)
- For each risk: probability, impact, owner, mitigation, trigger for escalation
- No risk slides without a mitigation bullet.

### 9. Asks: the only slide that must be yes/no
Board can ignore commentary. They cannot ignore a clear vote needed. Structure each ask as:
- What we need
- Why we need it
- What happens if we don’t get it
- Deadline for decision

---

## Verification Step

**The Red-Pen Test**

Print the deck (or export to PDF). With a red pen, read only the **slide titles** and the **first bullet** of each slide. If you can reconstruct the entire story, board decisions, and financial health without opening any charts, the deck passes. If not, rewrite titles until they are insights, not descriptions.

**The 5-Minute Board Test**
Hand the deck to someone who wasn’t in the data meetings. Five minutes later, ask: 1) What is the company’s health? 2) What are the biggest risks? 3) What does the board need to do? If they can’t answer all three, the deck failed.
