# B2B Forecast Call Discipline

## 1. Use 4 forecast categories (not pipeline stages)

| Category | Meaning | Rough probability |
|---|---|---|
| **Closed** | Won, signed, in production | 100% |
| **Commit** | Rep personally guarantees it will close this period | 80-90% |
| **Best Case** | Real, but missing one or two known items | 50-70% |
| **Upside** | Possible, but not yet qualified as commit | 20-40% |

## 2. Stage definitions must be binary

A stage is only complete when an **objective event** has happened. Opinions
are not stages.

| Stage | Entry criteria |
|---|---|
| Discovery | Business problem validated with budget owner |
| Solution fit | Technical/functional requirements mapped |
| Decision process | Evaluation criteria and timeline confirmed |
| Procurement | Legal, security, procurement engaged |
| Closed-won | Signed order/MSA + first invoice sent |

## 3. Inspection questions per deal

For every Commit or Best Case deal, ask:

1. What is the customer's compelling event / why buy now?
2. Who is the economic buyer and what is their personal win?
3. What is the decision process and date?
4. What is the single biggest remaining risk?
5. What would make this slip to next quarter?
6. What is the documented next step with date + owner?

If any answer is vague, downgrade one forecast category.

## 4. Forecast call cadence

| Meeting | Audience | Frequency | Duration |
|---|---|---|---|
| Rep 1:1 | Rep + manager | Weekly | 30 min |
| Region rollup | Managers + VP Sales | Weekly | 60 min |
| Executive forecast | CRO + CFO + CEO | Monthly or bi-weekly | 90 min |
| Board pipeline | CRO + board | Quarterly | 60 min |

## 5. Commit hygiene rules

- A rep can only **Commit** deals that have a scheduled closing action.
- No single deal > 20% of the commit number.
- Commit number should be **>80%** achieved by week 3 of the quarter.
- If a deal slips out of commit twice, it cannot be Commit again this quarter.

## 6. Common pitfalls

- **Happy ears**: rep believes the customer without evidence.
- **Sandbagging**: rep keeps won deals out of forecast to game quota.
- **Phantom upside**: old opportunities that should be closed-lost.
- **Stage inflation**: deal marked "procurement" without legal engaged.

## 7. Verification

After the call, produce:

- Commit number and coverage ratio vs quota.
- List of deals that moved category and why.
- List of deals that need executive intervention.
- Updated close plan for each Commit deal.
