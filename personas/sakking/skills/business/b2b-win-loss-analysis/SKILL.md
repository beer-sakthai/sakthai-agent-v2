# B2B Win-Loss Analysis

## 1. Capture every deal

Record outcome for every opportunity that reaches stage 2+ (qualified):

| Field | Why it matters |
|---|---|
| Win/Loss | Outcome |
| Primary competitor | Who did you compete against? |
| Loss reason | Buyer-stated reason |
| Deal size | Segment signal |
| Sales cycle length | Process friction |
| Discovery quality | Did you map the buying committee? |
| Champion strength | Internal advocate level |

## 2. Build a loss-code taxonomy

Keep it short (5-8 codes):

1. **Price** — priced above budget or perceived value.
2. **Product gap** — missing feature or integration.
3. **Competitor** — lost to named competitor.
4. **No decision** — customer did nothing.
5. **Timing** — budget/priority delayed.
6. **Trust** — security, brand, or reference concerns.
7. **Internal** — our own execution failure.

## 3. Run buyer interviews

Interview 3-5 buyers per quarter, both wins and losses:

- Use a third party or neutral internal person.
- Keep interviews to 20 minutes.
- Ask: Why did you buy / not buy? What alternatives did you evaluate? What
  was the deciding factor? What would have changed your mind?

## 4. Analyze the data

| Lens | Question |
|---|---|
| By segment | Are we losing more in SMB or Enterprise? |
| By competitor | Which competitor beats us where? |
| By loss reason | Is price the real reason, or a symptom? |
| By AE | Is one rep's pattern hiding a training gap? |

## 5. Convert insight to action

| Pattern | Likely fix |
|---|---|
| Price losses in enterprise | Add ROI calculator, value-based packaging |
| Product gap losses | Roadmap input + competitive positioning |
| No-decision losses | Improve discovery / urgency creation |
| Trust losses | Security page, references, case studies |
| Timing losses | Nurture cadence + budget-cycle marketing |

## 6. Pitfalls

- Letting sales reps self-report loss reasons (bias toward price).
- Only interviewing wins.
- Not sharing findings across product, marketing, and CS.
- Collecting data without a quarterly review ritual.

## 7. Output template

```markdown
## Win-Loss QX-YYYY

- Wins: N | Losses: N | No decision: N
- Top competitor: ___
- Top loss reason: ___
- Top win reason: ___
- Recommended actions:
  1. ...
  2. ...
```
